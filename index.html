From cylinders to complex shapes: Nematic Multi-Stable Shape-Morphing Cylindrical Actuators

Extended Abstract Code (soon)

Abstract

Cylindrical shape-morphing actuators are central to soft robotics, space, and medical applications subject to severe geometric constraints, yet achieving large, programmable, and stable deformations without continuous actuation remains challenging. We present a new class of cylindrical multi-stable nematic metamaterial actuators that combine director-field-based inverse design with compliant bi-stable mechanisms. The actuators are algorithmically designed from compliant bi-stable elements on a constant-thickness cylindrical shell cutout and fabricated as single-part structures, enabling rapid manufacturing via 3D printing or laser cutting without assembly. The proposed framework extends nematic shape-morphing to initially cylindrical geometries while embedding structural multi-stability to maintain deployed configurations. Numerical results demonstrate the ability to morph into a wide range of target shapes, and preliminary experiments validate the feasibility and robustness of the approach, opening new opportunities for soft robotic and deployable systems.
